Our Projects

Economical Vertical Farm

Queen’s Vertical Farming team aims to design and build a low budget, low operating cost farm that can be used on the Queen’s campus in Kingston, Ontario. The goal of this farm is to make vertical farming more accessible to more low- income communities to build more across Kingston and Ontario. QVFT aims to learn efficient and economical growing techniques to better improve the plant quality and budget for vertical farming. The budget goal of this farm is less than $5000 which all materials, nutrients and plants should be purchased below this threshold.

Muise Labs Vertical Farm

In April 2021, the Queen’s Vertical Farming Team became partners with Professors Christian Muise and Professor Ting Hu. With this partnership, QVFT has designed a vertical farm system comprising of 16 different zones where different plant or other variables can be controlled to change the outcome of the plant growth. The vertical farm system takes inspiration from other vertical farms in Canada and looks to incorporate emerging technologies such as machine learning and artificial intelligence to better grow various plants. This farm will act as a research hub for the Muise Lab graduate students.
